Abstract

Flow-matching models have enabled high-quality text-to-speech synthesis, but their iterative sampling process incurs substantial computational cost[cite: 59]. We introduce StepFlow, a modular distillation framework for one-step synthesis[cite: 62]. StepFlow addresses process variance caused by endpoint error accumulation and structural parameter inefficiency when reusing continuous-time architectures[cite: 60, 61]. By combining Dual Supervision with Step-Aware Architecture, StepFlow achieves near-teacher quality in a single inference step while reducing model parameters by 24%[cite: 64, 65, 66].

Proposed Methodology

Figure 1. Overview of the StepFlow framework. The framework transitions from a heavy MLP-based modulation network to a Step-Aware Architecture[cite: 274]. By utilizing Step-Aware Tokens, we align model capacity with the finite information entropy of the discrete task, reducing structural over-parameterization[cite: 81, 392]. On the right, our Dual Supervision strategy synergizes endpoint matching with deterministic mean velocity alignment, guiding the student along the teacher's mean trajectory and reducing training variance by 60%[cite: 275, 312].
